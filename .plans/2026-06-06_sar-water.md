## Goal

`sar_water(bbox, date?, windowDays?, thresholdDb?, orbitDirection?)` — quantify the
water-covered fraction of an AOI from Sentinel-1 VV backscatter (water/smooth = low γ⁰), the
all-weather flood/water-extent signal. Returns water % + validity + provenance.

## Background

ROADMAP Horizon 1 item 2 includes "flood/water mapping". Water specularly reflects radar away
from the sensor → very low VV backscatter, so a threshold on VV γ⁰ is a robust water mask that
works through cloud and at night. Reuses the Statistics API: a binary water evalscript whose
**mean = water fraction**. Builds on this session's multi-collection client generalization.

## Approach

- `sarWaterEvalscript(threshLinear)`: output `data` = `VV < thresh ? 1 : 0` (FLOAT32) +
  `dataMask`. Statistics' `mean` of that band = water fraction; `validPct` = data coverage.
- Generalize `statistics()` the same way `process()` was: accept `source?: DataSourceSpec`,
  default `s2Source()`. SAR water passes the `sentinel-1-grd` spec (GAMMA0 terrain-corrected,
  mostRecent). Threshold supplied in dB (interpretable) → linear via `10^(dB/10)`.
- Provenance via `sarProvenance`; the result records `thresholdDb` for reproducibility.
